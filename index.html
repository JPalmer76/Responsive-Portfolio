About Me

Well, as you probably know by now, judging by the large heading above, My Name is Jon Palmer. I was born in Northridge, CA on Arpil the 8th, 1976. I lived in Sunny, Southern California for most of my life. Some may feel that I was spoiled as a child because of this...I had a pretty great childhood!

I learned to play music in the 6th grade, and took to it pretty naturally, though no one in my immediate family played at all. I learned to play the upright bass, and played in an orchestra for 3 years, then switch to electric bass when I moved into high school. There, I was pulled into the high school jazz band. I had fun, but my heart was never into it, as I was always watching the drummer and wishing I was sitting at his instrument. Well, years later, I got my chance and it is an absolutly crazy story!! Which I will have to tell another time....

I started, and played in a band professionally starting in 1995, and we got signed to DreamWorks music in 1999. We released our first record in 2000 and instantly had a hit single. You may not know the band (most people don't know them by name), Lifehouse, but you would know the fisrt hit song(Hangin by a Moment) for sure.

However, I got fired from the band in mid 2000, and ended up moving to Redding, CA and lived with mom and dad for a while so I could get back on my feet and make a plan. The entire 5 years prior wasn't a waste. I met the woman I'd spend the rest of my life with during that time, and married her in 2001! We now have 3 teen age boys ranging in age from 17 to 14...My middle son is 15. I have been married for 18 years as of July 7th 2019. I love my family!!

That is a very high level view of my past, but that now brings us to the current day. I work for Louisiana Pacific Building Solutions. I've been in Marketing for about a year and a half now, and don't like it. I've been with the company for 5 years, and this is the first time in my tenure that I feel that I'm being forced to be fake(as marketers are). My brother in law went to a Galvanized Coding Bootcamp in Seattle, and has become a very successful software engineer. Also a musician, he and I think and work in a very similar way. I always used to think that coding was the work of geniuses, and there would be no way in hell that I'd ever be able to do that, but here I am!! With my brother in law's encouragement, and my determination to do something big, I am now here in Vanderbilt's Coding Bootcamp, and loving every bit of it. Here's to the future! CHEERS!
